================================================================================
          AI PROMPTS FOR GENERATING EASY-TO-SLICE SPRITE SHEETS & GRIDS
================================================================================

This file contains copy-pasteable prompt templates and guidelines to use with 
ChatGPT (DALL-E), Midjourney, or other AI image generators. They are optimized 
to produce sprite sheets that can be sliced cleanly by the Image Grid Slicer.

Choose the style that matches your needs:

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
STYLE 1: GRID WITH CROSSHAIR MARKERS (Best for Crosshair Grid Detection)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This style requests the AI to draw black '+' crosshair symbols between the grid
cells (just like in unnamed.png). Our crosshair auto-detection pass will align
the grid lines perfectly using these markers.

PROMPT TEMPLATE:
"Generate a 2D game sprite sheet of [DESCRIBE YOUR CHARACTER/ITEM HERE, e.g., a 
fantasy knight performing a sword swing animation]. 
Format the image as a uniform grid of 3 rows and 4 columns (12 frames total).
CRITICAL GRID INSTRUCTIONS:
1. The background of the entire image must be pure solid white.
2. Every individual cell in the grid must be separated by small, thin, black '+' 
   crosshair alignment marks drawn precisely at the intersections of the grid 
   boundaries (including the outer boundary edges).
3. The sprites in each frame must be identical in size, perfectly centered 
   within their respective grid cells, and must not touch the crosshair marks or 
   the boundaries.
4. Keep the visual style clean, flat, and consistent across all frames."

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
STYLE 2: SOLID GAP SPACING (Best for Standard Border Auto-Detection)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This style requests a solid, uniform background color with equal spacing gaps 
between the rows and columns. Our standard auto-detect border logic works by 
finding these solid gaps.

PROMPT TEMPLATE:
"Create a 2D character sprite sheet for [DESCRIBE CHARACTER/ITEM, e.g., a pixel 
art wizard walking].
Format the image into a perfectly aligned grid of 3 rows and 4 columns.
CRITICAL LAYOUT INSTRUCTIONS:
1. The background must be a single, solid, uniform flat color (use either pure 
   white #FFFFFF, pure black #000000, or a solid neon chroma-key green). There 
   must be absolutely no gradients, shadows, textures, or noise in the background.
2. Leave a clear, uniform spacing gap (gutter/margin) of approximately 20 pixels 
   between each row and column.
3. Every sprite must be completely self-contained and perfectly centered in the 
   middle of its cell, leaving ample empty background padding around it.
4. No part of the sprites should overlap or spill into the spacing gaps."

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
STYLE 3: SPRITES WITH A COMPOSITED BACKGROUND (Animation Layering)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
If you want to use the "Animate" tab's background composition feature (where 
you composite sprites over a static background image), use this multi-step 
prompt sequence.

STEP 1: Generate the Background Image
"Generate a high-detail, static background scene for a 2D side-scrolling game. 
The scene should be [DESCRIBE SCENE, e.g., a spooky dark forest or a sci-fi 
corridor]. It should be flat, without any characters, items, or foreground 
elements. Use a 16:9 aspect ratio."

STEP 2: Generate the Sprites (Matching the Art Style)
"Generate a 2D game sprite sheet of [DESCRIBE CHARACTER] that matches the art 
style of [DESCRIBE STEP 1 SCENE]. 
Format the image as a 3x4 grid of 12 frames. 
Every sprite frame must be centered on a pure, solid flat black background. 
Each sprite should be isolated, centered, and have empty space around it so 
the background can be keyed out/made transparent."

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
💡 PRO-TIPS FOR BEST RESULTS:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Avoid Gradients in the Background: DALL-E loves to add subtle lighting gradients 
   (vignettes) to the edges. Always specify "flat color background" or "solid 
   pure white" to ensure the gaps remain mathematically uniform.
2. Centering: Asking for "perfectly centered within its respective grid cell" 
   is key to preventing cropped or off-center animations when you loop the frames.
3. Color Contrasting: Choose a background color that is not present in the sprite 
   itself (e.g., use a white background for dark sprites, and a black background 
   for bright/neon sprites). This makes transparency keying much cleaner.
